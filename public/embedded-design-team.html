What is an embedded design team?

An embedded design team is a group of senior product designers who work inside your company like in-house staff — in your planning, tools, and delivery cycles — without recruiting or growing permanent headcount.

Ours comes with AI leverage built in: a compact senior team that delivers the output of a much larger one.

How embedding works

From scoping call to shipping cadence

Embedding follows our decision-led design process: the team plugs into your rituals, works visibly, and designs ahead of engineering.

  1. 01
    Scope & match

    We map your roadmap, stack, and rituals, then shape the team — typically a lead product designer plus the specialists your backlog needs.

  2. 02
    Plug in

    The team joins your Slack, Figma, and Linear or Jira — and your planning. First tickets move within days, not after a month of onboarding.

  3. 03
    Ship every cycle

    Design lands ahead of engineering each sprint. Work stays visible in one shared space, and decisions get made in short loops, not weekly syncs.

  4. 04
    Scale up or down

    Capacity flexes with the roadmap — add a specialist for a heavy quarter, scale down after launch. No recruiting cycle to undo, no severance math.

FAQ

What clients ask us most

How fast can an embedded design team start?

Days, not quarters. We scope your roadmap and stack in one call, match the team, and the first tickets are usually in motion within the first week — while a comparable in-house hire is still in the second interview round.

Who actually works on our product?

Named senior product designers, led by a design lead — the same people every sprint, not a rotating bench. AI tools multiply their output, but every decision and every screen is owned by a designer you know by name.

What tools and rituals do you join?

Yours. Figma, Slack, Linear, Jira, Notion — plus your planning, standups, and reviews where it makes sense. Embedding means we adapt to your process; you don't adopt ours.

How is this different from an agency retainer?

A retainer sends tickets over a wall and gets designs back. An embedded design team sits inside your planning: it hears the context, joins the trade-off discussions, and designs ahead of engineering — so decisions happen in hours, not in weekly syncs.

Can we scale the team up or down?

Yes, month to month. Add a specialist for a heavy quarter, scale down after a launch. No recruiting cycle to undo, no severance math — capacity follows the roadmap.

Does the embedded team replace our in-house designers?

No — it extends them. We often pair with an in-house design lead who owns direction, while the embedded team adds senior production capacity and keeps the design system consistent across everything that ships.

How much does an embedded design team cost?

One predictable monthly fee, shaped by the team's size and seniority. With AI leverage, a compact senior team typically delivers the output of a much larger traditional one — here's the math behind AI-augmented product design cost.
