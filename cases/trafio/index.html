Overview

Money, made legible.

Traflo set out to fix a quiet frustration: banking apps show you numbers but rarely help you understand them. Balances without context, transactions without meaning, goals buried in menus. The product promise was a calmer, more human way to manage everyday money.

Our job was to turn that promise into an interface where the most important question - how am I doing? - is answered the second the app opens.

The challenge

In banking, clarity is the product.

A finance app earns trust in the small moments: checking a balance, understanding a charge, watching a goal move. If those feel confusing, no feature list saves it.

The work focused on making the balance meaningful, making transactions readable, and keeping goals in sight - progress people can feel, not hunt for.

Our approach

AI explores. Designers decide.

01 Frame

AI digested competitor teardowns and patterns in where people lose confidence in finance apps. Designers turned it into one decision: Traflo wins or loses at three moments - checking the balance, understanding a transaction, tracking a goal.

02 Explore

AI generated navigation models, home-screen architectures, and transaction-feed patterns. Designers narrowed hard around one principle: clarity has a rhythm. Every screen answers one question first, then offers depth on demand.

03 Prototype

Core flows became clickable early, including empty accounts, failed payments, mid-month budget warnings, and edge-case balances. Two obvious features failed testing and never reached the backlog.

04 Scale

The approved direction became a design system - components, tokens, interaction patterns, and documentation - so every new screen assembled from the system instead of starting over.
