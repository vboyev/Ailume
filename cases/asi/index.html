Overview

Tutoring, the moment a student needs it.

ASI gives students help the second they are stuck - not the next school day. It is a conversational AI tutor that explains, quizzes, and tracks mastery, meeting learners where they already spend time.

Our job was the part students actually touch: an interface that feels less like a chatbot and more like a tutor who knows them. And just as important, a system disciplined enough to grow with the roadmap.

The challenge

AI tutoring needs trust, rhythm, and clear feedback.

A model that can answer anything still fails if students do not trust it, cannot feel their progress, or get lost in a wall of text. The interface had to do three jobs at once and stay coherent as the product expanded.

Answers had to feel guiding, practice had to feel light and motivating, and progress had to read at a glance.

Our approach

AI explores. Designers decide.

01 Frame

AI compressed the groundwork - a teardown of leading chat and tutoring apps plus patterns in how students disengage - into a clear map. Designers set the spine: ASI had to feel like a tutor.

02 Explore

AI generated chat layouts, onboarding flows, and gamification models. Designers kept the direction that guided students toward understanding instead of dumping answers.

03 Prototype

Tutor setup, live session, achievements, multiplayer quiz, typing, loading, errors, and empty states became clickable early, so awkward moments were fixed while cheap to change.

04 Scale

Interactive components, tokens, chat patterns, and animation specs were handed to engineering build-ready, so new subjects, game modes, and platforms could assemble from existing parts.
