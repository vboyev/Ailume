About the project

A complex workflow product needed one visual and interaction language.

We redesigned the core platform experience around repeatable patterns, clear hierarchy, and a design system that could keep growing after the first release.

The result is a product interface that feels coherent across dashboards, lists, integrations, permissions, and team collaboration states.

Challenge

Workflow products break when every edge case gets its own UI rule.

FlowApps needed reusable logic for dense screens, states, cards, tables, dropdowns, and collaboration controls, while still keeping the interface approachable for daily users.

Our process

We designed the system around reusable decisions, not isolated screens.

We mapped product areas, clustered recurring states, explored UI directions, and translated the chosen direction into components, rules, and screen examples ready for engineering.

Results

A cleaner product surface with fewer design bottlenecks.

The FlowApps team received a scalable interface foundation that shortened future screen creation and made product expansion easier to manage.
