Overview

A scalable workflow tool without visual overload.

FlowApps had to support managers, contributors, reporting, and a growing ecosystem of integrations without becoming visually exhausting.

We designed the platform around progressive density: calm defaults first, deeper information only when it helps the task at hand.

Challenge

Complex products usually break at the hierarchy layer.

The system needed to feel lightweight for day-to-day execution while still giving managers meaningful control and visibility.

That meant solving navigation, status, permissions, and integrations as one coherent structure rather than as separate feature buckets.

Approach

AI sped up exploration across a wide product surface.

01 Frame

Prioritize the essential flows

We reduced the product to the journeys that matter most: manager setup, teammate task completion, and cross-team oversight.

02 Explore

Test multiple density models

AI helped fan out dashboard, list, and board directions so designers could converge quickly on the clearest hierarchy.

03 Validate

Prototype the heavy parts early

Permissions, empty states, workload views, and integration paths became clickable before the team committed to build.

04 Systemize

Turn approved UI into product rules

The final interface became a reusable library of components, states, and handoff guidance for future modules.
