Overview

Depth for managers. Calm for everyone.

Project management tools have a built-in trap: every feature you add for managers makes the product heavier for the people doing the work. Add 50+ integrations on top, and most tools collapse into clutter.

FlowApps wanted both ends at once: the control managers need and the simplicity that keeps a team inside the product every day.

The challenge

Information density is where this product wins or loses.

The hard question was not what features - it was how much any one screen should say at once. Too little and managers feel limited; too much and everyone else bounces.

The interface had to serve two audiences, one wide product surface, and a roadmap where new modules could not mean a new design language each time.

Our approach

AI explores. Designers decide.

01 Frame

AI processed workflow patterns across leading PM tools, churn drivers from public reviews, and the client feature map. Designers chose the moments worth winning: a manager first setup and a teammate first task.

02 Explore

AI generated dashboard models, navigation systems, and board layouts. Designers chose progressive density: a calm default view with detail one deliberate step away.

03 Prototype

Core journeys went clickable fast - building a team, assigning roles, clearing a day, reading hours across a sprint - including empty boards, permission conflicts, and the 50-integration settings screen.

04 Scale

The approved direction became a design system with components, tokens, status patterns, and dev-ready handoff that absorbs new modules without redesign.
