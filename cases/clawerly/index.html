Overview

The real competitor is giving up.

Clawerly needed more than clean lessons. It needed an experience that makes progress visible enough for learners to keep going.

We designed the platform so every next action feels clear, every return feels possible, and momentum never hides behind the interface.

Challenge

Motivation is a product problem before it becomes a retention problem.

Most language tools do not lose users because they lack content. They lose them when progress stops feeling real and the next lesson feels heavier than it should.

Our task was to design around plateaus, missed days, and moments of doubt without making the product feel over-managed.

Approach

AI accelerated pattern search. Designers shaped the learning rhythm.

01 Frame

Map the momentum leaks

We turned engagement research and product teardown work into a clear set of drop-off moments: stalled progress, repetitive lessons, and awkward returns.

02 Explore

Test visible progress models

AI helped broaden progress structures, path maps, and reinforcement ideas so designers could quickly refine what feels motivating rather than noisy.

03 Validate

Prototype the comeback flows early

We tested first lessons, missed-day recovery, skill summaries, and harder practice moments before committing the product structure.

04 Systemize

Build a reusable learning UI

The approved direction became a system of lesson, reward, and progress components that can scale across future formats.
