Overview

The real competitor is quitting.

Nobody churns from a language app because it lacks features. They churn because motivation runs out: progress feels invisible, lessons feel the same, and the finish line looks far away.

Clawerly promise - interactive lessons, expert teachers, adaptive paths - only matters if the interface keeps that momentum alive. Our job was to design a platform where the next lesson always feels worth starting.

The challenge

Motivation is a design problem, not a feature.

Every screen either feeds momentum or quietly kills it. The hard moments are the plateaus, the returns after a break, and the times progress stops being visible.

The product had to make progress feel real, keep lessons light, and win the comeback when a lapsed learner decides whether to return.

Our approach

AI explores. Designers decide.

01 Frame

AI synthesized engagement patterns, drop-off research, and learner reviews into a map of where motivation leaks. Designers turned that into three momentum loops: finish a lesson, see progress move, want the next one.

02 Explore

AI generated progress models, lesson-flow structures, streaks, path maps, and skill trees. Designers kept what respected the learner: visible path plus proof of skill.

03 Prototype

Core journeys went clickable fast - first lesson, return after a missed week, level-up, hard exercise - including empty streaks, failed tests, and the behind moment that usually triggers churn.

04 Scale

The approved direction became a design system where new lesson formats assemble from existing parts instead of new screens.
